Green giving this holiday season

   

Holiday shopping does a lot of damage to the environment – not to mention our wallets. Instead, consider eco-friendly alternatives like re-gifting, buying second-hand, creating handmade presents or giving experiences instead of objects. Remember: memories made together last far longer than the “stuff” that clutters our homes or ends up in the landfill.

The newest Take Action item from EcoKids, Go vintage!, will help children and families learn about one way to have a green holiday.

Beyond Green goes above and beyond!

   

The Beyond Green Environment Summit was a huge success. For three days in October, over 900 young Canadians, facilitators, organizations and inspiring environmental leaders connected at the University of Toronto to explore a diversity of environmental issues in hands-on workshops, speaker panels and break-out sessions.

Beyond Green was Canada’s largest and most dynamic environmental youth summit to date! The event sparked critical discussion and encouraged action across disciplines and perspectives to create environmental change. You can find photos of this game-changing Summit at earthday.ca/beyondgreen.

Going forward, we will be keeping the conversations and actions alive with resources, connections and meet-ups in 2014! Stay tuned at earthday.ca for all Beyond Green follow-ups.

Youth Activator Network takes the GTA by storm in 2014

   

The EcoMentors program is happy to announce that it will be ramping up Toronto area trainings in 2014 with its Youth Activator Network (YAN) – a group of three EcoMentors facilitators, who were selected from a wide pool of applicants to enhance our capacity to develop young environmental leaders. Initial YAN training sessions began in the fall of 2013 and will be continuing into the New Year.

From January until May 2014, seven EcoMentors training sessions will be offered in Central (Downtown), North Western (Etobicoke and North York) and Eastern (Scarborough) Toronto regions. These city-wide sessions will increase our fast-growing network of environmentally engaged young people, who will deliver workshops on issues to school groups and peers in their local communities.

Heroic group hard at work with $10,000 award from the Hometown Heroes Award Program

   

Hometown Heroes Group Award Winner 2013, Lower Mainland Green Team.

The Lower Mainland Green Team (LMGT) has used their $10,000 cash-prize to build activities focused on engaging youth (with a focus on at-risk youth) in experiential environmental hands-on learning. Since being awarded the Hometown Heroes Group Award in June 2013, LMGT has run nine youth-focused stewardship and habitat restoration events, engaging 205 youth in planting 1,545 native plants and trees, and removing 450 pounds of invasive plants. Winning the Hometown Heroes Award has allowed LMGT to continue to make a long-term impact in their region and provide youth with opportunities to take an active role in helping the environment.

The nomination deadline for Hometown Heroes 2014 is March 31, 2014. For more information on Hometown Heroes or to nominate an individual, group or small business from your community, please visit earthday.ca/hometown.

Introducing Earth Day Canada’s new President

   

Deborah Doncaster

After over 20 years as Earth Day Canada’s President, Jed is retiring. Though he will be missed, we are thrilled to announce that Deborah Doncaster will be taking over his position in early January!

Deborah Doncaster has spent over 20 years in the areas of human rights, animal welfare, environmental conservation, planning and stewardship, community power and green energy. She has helped build Canada’s first community-owned wind turbine on the Toronto Waterfront in 2004. While at TREC Renewable Energy Co-operative (TREC), Deb helped found the Ontario Sustainable Energy Association (OSEA), a member-based community organization that saw the TREC turbine as a symbol of hope that Ontario citizens could develop and own community-based, non-polluting electricity generation.  Under Deb’s leadership, OSEA’s efforts resulted in Ontario’s Renewable Energy Standard Offer Program in 2006, and the establishment of the Community Power Fund (CP Fund) in 2007. 

In 2008, she launched a multi-stakeholder campaign for a Green Energy Act for Ontario, which passed into law in 2009. For her work on this act, Deb was honoured with the City of Toronto’s Green Leadership Award for Market Transformation.

Deb holds Master’s degrees in Law from Carleton University (Ottawa) and Environmental Studies from York University (Toronto). She lives in Toronto with her husband Ben and son Devon.

The New Year is a great time to set new goals for ourselves. Here are some 2014 resolutions from our team!
